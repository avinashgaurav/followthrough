1. Realized Savings vs. Recommendation Potential
    1.​ Brightwater highlighted that realized savings lag recommendation generation, with a typical
        realization cycle of 1 - 2 quarters.
    2.​ Root Cause: XYZ provides recommendations, but the implementation team (a separate
        team at Brightwater) may delay or reject execution.
Key Insight
   1.​ Month 1 example: recommendation potential roughly $18K - the majority can be applied
        initially.
   2.​ Subsequent months: remaining optimizations shrink significantly to roughly $2 - 3K, mostly
        limited to disk optimization and snapshot cleanup.
Implication
   1.​ The savings curve is front-loaded, then tapers. This requires clear expectation-setting in
       dashboards and pricing models.

2. Need for Turnaround Time (TAT) Tracking
Required Metrics
   1.​ Recommendation created date.
   2.​ Recommendation sent-to-implementation-team date.
   3.​ Date of execution.
   4.​ TAT (execution delay).
Additional Tracking
   1.​ Percentage of recommendations implemented / rejected / pending.
Purpose
   1.​ Measure execution efficiency, identify bottlenecks in implementation, and strengthen
       governance and reporting.

3. Pricing Model Discussion
    1.​ Suggested approach: a quarterly pricing model.
Rationale
   1.​ Real savings become visible by the second quarter, which allows for performance validation and
       pricing adjustments post-value realization.

4. Onboarding Timeline Expectations
   1.​ MSME clients: approximately 45 days.
   2.​ Enterprise clients: approximately 90 days.
   3.​ Implication: Sales and onboarding timelines must be factored into revenue realization
       forecasting.

5. Product and Feature Feedback
Positive Feedback

Operational Constraints
   1.​ No active use of autoscaling at Brightwater.
Production Setup
   1.​ Typical architecture includes Primary, Secondary and Disaster Recovery resources.
Implication
   1.​ Recommendations must respect redundancy setups and avoid aggressive downsizing in DR /
       secondary layers.

7. Key Takeaways - Blunt Assessment
   1.​ The current savings narrative is misleading if it does not show decay over time.
   2.​ Without TAT tracking, the product looks like a recommendation engine rather than a system of
    action.
3.​ Pricing must align with realized savings (not potential) and with the time lag (1 - 2 quarters).
4.​ If implementation is external, accountability layers must be built, otherwise ROI attribution
    breaks.
