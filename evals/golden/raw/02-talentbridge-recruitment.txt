1. Infrastructure Context Gathered
   1.​ Cloud Provider: Exclusively AWS; no Azure, GCP or multi-cloud footprint at present.
   2.​ Total Spend: Monthly cloud spend was estimated at INR 15,000 - 20,000, roughly USD 180 - 240
       at prevailing rates.
   3.​ Spend Breakdown: Of this total, approximately INR 12,000 per month was attributable to
       non-production and maintenance workloads - a very high proportion, which made TalentBridge a
       naturally strong fit for XYZ's scheduler-driven savings model.
   4.​ Decision Stack: Anjali's role is budget oversight; the technical infrastructure decisions (VMs vs.
       Kubernetes, instance sizing, etc.) are made by a dedicated architect. This shaped the demo to
       emphasize outcomes and savings rather than low-level configuration mechanics.

2. XYZ Day vs. XYZ Clarification
Anjali asked for clarification on the difference between the two products in the XYZ portfolio. The
positioning was conveyed as follows:
   1.​ XYZ Day: Deployment and production-readiness tool. It stands up CI/CD pipelines and
       observability to make an application production-ready in roughly 30 minutes.
   2.​ XYZ: Cloud cost optimization tool that intelligently shuts down resources when they are not
       in use and surfaces savings recommendations. The focus of this call was XYZ.
3. Feature Walkthrough - Tailored for Maintenance Cost Optimization
A. Automated Resource Sync
    1.​ Once AWS credentials are connected, XYZ automatically lists every resource (VMs,
        databases, etc.) and applies its own internal tagging via XYZ, without interfering with any
        existing AWS tags.
B. Visual Scheduling via Heat-Map
    1.​ Active (green) and stopped (red) hours can be painted onto a weekly grid through the UI.
    2.​ This is the primary lever for shutting down non-prod environments overnight and on weekends -
        directly relevant to the INR 12,000 / month non-prod bucket identified above.
C. Schedule Overrides
    1.​ If extra work is needed on a given day, the schedule can be overridden for a set number of hours
        without mutating the permanent schedule.
D. Budgetary Controls
    1.​ Budgets can be assigned to individual teams or resource groups.
    2.​ Alert thresholds (for instance, 75% of monthly budget consumed) trigger proactive warnings in
        configured channels.
E. Centralized Visibility
    1.​ The Overview dashboard shows total savings, team performance, and resource counts. The
        demo sandbox surfaced over $1,800 of savings in a mock environment, which provided a
        quantitative anchor for discussion.
F. Governance, Audit and Recommendations
    1.​ Granular role-based access control plus color-coded audit logs make it possible to track exactly
        who changed the state of which resource and when.
    2.​ The recommendations engine continuously scans for idle resources and proposes specific
        remediation steps to reduce cost further.

4. Observed Fit
    1.​ Given that the majority of TalentBridge's AWS spend (roughly 60-80%) is concentrated in
        non-production / maintenance workloads, the XYZ scheduler directly targets the most
        expensive line item in the account.
    2.​ The size of the estate is modest, so the conversation naturally centered on clear ROI rather than
        large enterprise-scale governance workflows.
